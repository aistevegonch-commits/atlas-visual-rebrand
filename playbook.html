Section 02

Market Reality

You are not competing with Hollywood. You are fighting for a tiny slice of a $31B market against solo operators, agencies, and iPhones.

Market Size Context

The US video production market is ~$31.2B. But that includes everything — Hollywood, corporate, TikTok, social media. Real estate video is a niche within a niche. No major research firm tracks it separately. Clark County, WA is not LA. Vancouver's average home price is ~$450K. The budgets reflect that.

ℹ COVID Changed the Game — Permanently
Virtual tours went from novelty to expectation during COVID and stuck. NAR 2025 Technology Survey: 52% of agents use drone photography/video. Video is now table stakes for $1M+ listings in major metros. In secondary markets like Clark County, adoption lags — which means opportunity, but also education costs.

⚠ Opportunity or Warning Sign?
No dedicated RE video production companies showed up in search for Vancouver/Clark County specifically. No competition could mean no demand, or it could mean an unserved market.

The Agency vs Solo Operator Gap

Solo operators ($150–$1,500/project) compete on price, speed, and personal service. Their ceiling is time-for-money. Agency positioning ($3K–$15K/project) requires a professional website, portfolio, sales process, and brand trust. Most solo operators never cross this chasm. Clients with $10K+ budgets rarely hire individuals. Clients with $500 budgets rarely hire agencies.

AI Disruption Threat

AI video generation (Sora, Runway) is not yet replacing property tours, but it is replacing templated marketing videos. Virtual staging and 3D tours (Matterport) are commoditizing fast — $100 Matterport scans exist. The defendable niche: high-end cinematic storytelling and relationship-driven commercial work that requires on-site judgment and creative direction.

The 15% Rule

⚠ Gear Discipline
In Year 1, no single equipment purchase should exceed 15% of trailing 3-month revenue. If you made $22,500 in the last quarter, your gear budget cap is $3,375. Anything above that is gambling with survival cash.

Gear ROI Test

Before any purchase over $1,000, answer these three questions with numbers:

1. Will this enable charging 15%+ more on the next 6 projects?

Example: New cinema camera enables "4K 120fps slow-motion" as a billed add-on at $400/project. 6 × $400 = $2,400. Purchase price $5,500. Payback: 14 projects.

Marginal — verify against actual client demand

2. Will this reduce production time by 20%+ on every project?

Example: New computer renders 40% faster. Saves 3 hrs/project × 30 projects × $95/hr = $8,550/year. Purchase price $3,200. Payback: 4.5 months.

Smart Buy

3. Does a paying client specifically require this capability?

Example: Client requires RAW 6K delivery. Current camera does not. Client revenue = $12,000. Purchase justified by confirmed revenue.

Section 06

Client Acquisition — What Actually Works

Hope is not a strategy. Here are the channels, the math, and the honest close rates.

Referrals: 40–60% of Consistent Business

Real estate is relationship-driven. One agent refers another. Brokerage relationships are gold — land one Keller Williams or Windermere office, get 20 agents. Highest close rate (~50-70%), but unpredictable. Feast-famine source without systems.

Cold Email: 0.3–1.5% Close Rate

To land 1 client, you need to contact 70-300 qualified prospects. B2B cold email response rates are ~1-5%. Targeting 1-2 contacts per company yields 7.8% reply rates vs 3.8% when blasting 10+ people. Relentless follow-up (2-3 touches minimum) is non-negotiable.

LinkedIn Outbound System

  • Optimize personal profile: headline = "Cinematic property videos for developers" not "Videographer | Storyteller | Creative."
  • Create company page with portfolio and contact.
  • Post 2x/week: behind-the-scenes, before/after grades, market commentary + video example.
  • Send 15-20 connection requests/week to target titles with personalized notes.
  • Engage on target companies' posts before sending cold messages.

⚠ The Portfolio Trap Warning
Do not work for free. Do not discount Signature or Enterprise to "build portfolio." Portfolio quality improves faster when clients pay full price — they give better feedback, clearer direction, and higher stakes that force growth. Discounted clients ($800 Essential projects) refer other discount-seeking clients. Your network becomes a pyramid of price sensitivity. If more than 30% of Year 1 projects are priced below $2,000, you are in the trap. Abort immediately and raise rates.
Section 07

Operational Workflows

Amateurs wing it. Professionals run systems. Here are the systems.

Pre-Production

  • Discovery Call (30 min): Purpose, audience, placement, style, timeline, budget.
  • Shot List / Creative Brief: Deliver 48 hours before shoot. Every required shot listed. Golden hour times, drone restrictions, access points.
  • Location Scout: Google Earth / DroneDeploy for access. Check power, parking, noise sources (HVAC, highways). Confirm key access.

Production Day: Solo Shooting Checklist

Before Leaving

  • Batteries charged (camera, gimbal, audio, phone)
  • Memory cards formatted (2× copies)
  • Lens cloth, ND filters
  • Shot list printed or on phone
  • Client contact saved
  • Insurance cert saved to phone

On Location

  • 360 walkthrough first; plan route
  • Shoot exteriors first (light changes)
  • Every shot: 10-second minimum head and tail
  • B-roll: wide, medium, detail, movement
  • Record 30 seconds room tone per location
  • Backup cards to portable SSD between setups

Backup Protocol (Non-Negotiable)

⚠ One Drive Failure = Bankruptcy
Dual card recording if camera supports it (FX30 does). At lunch/break: offload Card A to SSD #1, Card B to SSD #2. Never travel with all media in one bag. Do not erase cards until 3 copies exist. Keep client projects for 1 year minimum.

Post-Production Workflow

  1. Ingest: Copy footage, verify integrity, rename files.
  2. Proxy Creation: If underpowered machine, create proxies in Resolve.
  3. Assembly Cut: Rough story structure. No color, no sound mix.
  4. Fine Cut: Pacing, music, graphics. Match frame rates and resolutions.
  5. Color Grade: Show LUT, shot-by-shot correction, stylized grade.
  6. Sound Design: Mix dialogue, ambient, music levels duck under speech.
  7. Graphics: Lower thirds, end card with CTA.
  8. Export: Master ProRes 422 HQ. Deliverables H.264/H.265 MP4 (4K + 1080p).
  9. Archive: Move to backup drives. Clear working drive after client approval.

Revision and Change Orders

Standard contract includes 2 revision rounds:

  • Round 1: Structural (reorder scenes, swap music, trim sections).
  • Round 2: Polish (text changes, color tweak, audio level).
  • Round 3+: Billed at $100-150/hour or flat $300/round. More than 3 rounds = change order.
