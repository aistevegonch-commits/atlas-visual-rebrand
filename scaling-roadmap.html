Phase 0

Pre-Launch

Before Day 1. Be ready to operate as a business on Day 1, not a freelancer with a camera.

Goal: LLC formed, website live, 5+ warm conversations scheduled

Week 1-2: Legal & Identity

  • Form LLC + EIN
  • Business checking account
  • Insurance (BOP + E&O, ~$1,200/year)
  • Contract templates (client + subcontractor)
  • Domain + Google Workspace
  • Set up accounting (QuickBooks SE, $30/mo)

Week 3-4: Digital Presence

  • Website live (use existing index.html)
  • LinkedIn company page
  • Portfolio/demo reel (even if spec work)
  • Vimeo/YouTube portfolio channel
  • Business cards
  • Email signature + brand templates

Week 5-6: Systems & Tools

  • Frame.io for client review
  • Artlist/Epidemic Sound subscription
  • CRM (HubSpot free)
  • Editor onboarding kit (LUTs, style guide, templates)
  • Proposal template
  • Invoicing system

Week 7-8: Network Prep

  • List 50 real estate contacts
  • Identify 100 LinkedIn targets
  • Join NAIOP, ULI, local realtor associations
  • Prepare warm outreach scripts
  • Schedule 5-10 intro coffees

Phase 1

Launch

Days 1-90. Close first clients, validate pricing, build portfolio.

Goal: $3,000-8,000 revenue, 1+ paying client, 2+ testimonials

Month 1: Foundation + Warm Network

  • Execute 90-Day Plan Phase 1
  • Target: 5+ intro conversations, website live
  • Budget: $500 (tools, mailers)

Month 2: Cold Outreach + Content

  • Execute 90-Day Plan Phase 2
  • 20 cold emails/week, 2 LinkedIn posts/week
  • Film 2-3 spec videos
  • Target: 3+ discovery calls, 1+ proposal sent
  • Budget: $800 (mailers, LinkedIn Premium, spec shoots)

Month 3: First Closes

  • Execute 90-Day Plan Phase 3
  • Close 1-3 projects at "founding client" pricing (30% below market)
  • Collect testimonials + case studies
  • Document what worked
  • Target: $3,000-8,000 revenue, 2+ testimonials
  • Budget: $700 (editor costs, travel)

Phase 2

Validation

Months 4-6. Prove repeatable client acquisition and project delivery.

Goal: $32,000-55,000 cumulative revenue (Q2), close rate >15%, 1+ repeat client

Month 4: Pricing Correction

  • Raise prices to market rate (no more founding discounts)
  • Target: 2-3 projects at full rate
  • Begin contract editor relationship (if >3 projects/month)
  • Focus: Signature tier ($3,500-7,500)
  • Revenue target: $8,000-15,000

Month 5: Repeatability

  • Systematize outreach: same volume every week
  • Build editor bench (2-3 reliable editors)
  • First retainer conversation with repeat client
  • Target: 3-4 projects
  • Revenue target: $12,000-20,000

Month 6: Cash Flow Stability

  • Deposit + final payment rhythm established
  • 3-month expense buffer achieved (~$15,000 cumulative cash)
  • Portfolio now has 5+ real projects
  • Target: 3-4 projects
  • Revenue target: $12,000-20,000

Phase 3

Optimization

Months 7-12. Maximize margins, build systems, prepare for scale.

Goal: $80,000-150,000 cumulative revenue (Q3+Q4), net margin >20%, 1+ retainer

Month 7-8: Margin Focus

  • Stop taking Essential-tier projects unless they lead to Signature/Enterprise
  • Negotiate editor rates down with volume
  • Add value-adds: social cutdowns, stills, drone (upsell)
  • Target: 3-4 projects/month
  • Revenue target: $15,000-25,000/month

Month 9-10: Systems & Automation

  • Proposal templates standardized
  • Client onboarding automated
  • CRM fully utilized
  • First retainer client secured ($2,500-4,000/month)
  • Target: 3-4 projects/month + 1 retainer
  • Revenue target: $18,000-28,000/month

Month 11-12: Year-End Review

  • Financial audit: what tiers were most profitable?
  • Client audit: who are the top 20% by revenue? Who are the bottom 20%?
  • Portfolio audit: which projects attracted the best clients?
  • Plan Year 2 budget and targets
  • Tax prep and estimated payments
  • Target: $15,000-25,000/month

Phase 4

Scale

Year 2. Transition from solo operator to micro-agency.

Goal: $250,000-400,000 annual revenue, net margin >25%, retainer revenue >40%

Q1 Year 2: First Hire

  • Trigger: 6-month trailing avg >$18,000/mo AND cash reserve >$25,000
  • Hire W-2 editor ($48K base + ~$6K loaded = $54K/year)
  • Owner transitions to sales + creative direction only
  • Target: $180,000-250,000 annual revenue

Q2 Year 2: Volume

  • Editor handles 60% of post-production
  • Owner sells full-time
  • Target: $220,000-300,000 annual revenue
  • Add second contract editor for overflow

Q3 Year 2: Retainer Base

  • 40% of revenue from retainers (stable, predictable)
  • Raise prices 15-20% (proven track record)
  • Target: $280,000-350,000 annual revenue

Q4 Year 2: Systems

  • Document all processes (SOPs)
  • Implement project management tool (Notion/Monday)
  • Hire part-time producer or account manager
  • Target: $300,000-400,000 annual revenue

Phase 5

Expansion

Year 3+. Geographic expansion and team growth.

Goal: $1M+ annual revenue, multiple regional hubs, full agency infrastructure

Year 3: Regional

  • Trigger: Local market generating >$250,000/year
  • Expand to Seattle (165 miles, 4 Signature projects to break even)
  • Hire second shooter/editor in new market
  • Target: $400,000-600,000 annual revenue
  • Team: 3-4 FTE

Year 4: Multi-Market

  • Add Bend or Spokane
  • Target: $600,000-900,000 annual revenue
  • Team: 5-7 FTE
  • Implement creative director role (you)

Year 5: National

  • Target: $1,000,000+ annual revenue
  • Team: 8-12 FTE
  • Multiple regional hubs
  • Full agency infrastructure

Common Failure Patterns by Phase

These are not hypotheticals. These are the reasons most video production businesses die.

Phase 1: Not Enough Outreach

Most people send 5 cold emails and quit. You need 20/week for 12 weeks. Volume is the only variable you control early on.

Phase 2: Pricing Too Low Too Long

The portfolio trap. First 3 projects can be discounted. Projects 4+ must be market rate. Every discount past #3 teaches the market you are cheap.

Phase 3: Editing Everything Yourself

You become a $75/hr editor instead of a $200/hr salesperson. Hire contract editors. Your job is to sell and direct, not to render.

Phase 4: Hiring Too Early

A W-2 editor costs $54K/year. If revenue drops, you fire them or go broke. Wait for 6 months of $18K+/mo before hiring.

Phase 5: Expanding Before Saturation

"I want to be national" is ego. "I want to own Portland" is strategy. Saturate one market before opening another.
