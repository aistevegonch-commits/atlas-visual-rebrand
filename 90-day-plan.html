Week 1: Legal & Identity

Form LLC in Washington State (file online, $200)
Obtain EIN from IRS (free, online)
Open business checking account
Register for WA B&O tax (1.5% on gross receipts)
Obtain Master Business License ($50–100)
Get liability insurance quote (BOP + E&O, ~$1,200/year)
Purchase domain (atlasvisual.co or similar, ~$15)
Set up Google Workspace ($12/month)
Create Atlas Visual logo (Canva Pro or hire designer, $50–200)
Set up QuickBooks or Wave for accounting

Week 2: Digital Presence

Build website (use existing index.html or Carrd/Squarespace)
Create LinkedIn company page
Set up Calendly for discovery calls
Create portfolio/demo reel (use existing work)
Set up Vimeo Pro or YouTube for portfolio hosting
Order business cards (Moo, $100)
Create email signature and brand templates

Week 3: Systems & Tools

Set up Frame.io account ($15/month)
Subscribe to Artlist or Epidemic Sound ($199/year)
Create editor onboarding kit (LUTs, style guide, templates)
Draft client services agreement template
Draft subcontractor/editor agreement template
Set up CRM (HubSpot free tier)
Create proposal template
Set up invoicing system (QuickBooks or Wave)

Week 4: Warm Network Blitz

List 50 people you know in real estate (agents, brokers, contractors, investors, property managers)
Send personal texts/emails to top 20
Use script: “Hey [Name], I just launched Atlas Visual — a video production company focused on helping developers and investment firms market properties. Do you know anyone who might need listing videos or brand content? I'd love an intro.”
Schedule 5–10 intro conversations (not sales pitches)
Post launch announcement on LinkedIn and personal social
Ask 3 past clients for testimonials
Phase 1 Budget: ~$1,500  |  Success Metric: 5+ warm conversations scheduled, website live, LLC formed

Weeks 5–6: LinkedIn Outbound System

Identify 100 target contacts on LinkedIn
Titles to target: Director of Development, VP Acquisitions, Asset Manager, Marketing Manager at multifamily/commercial firms
Send 20 personalized connection requests/week with note
Script: “Hi [Name], I produce cinematic property videos for developers in the PNW market. Would love to connect and share some work.”
Post 2x/week on LinkedIn (behind-the-scenes, before/after, market commentary)
Engage daily on target contacts' posts

Weeks 7–8: Cold Outreach + Content

Send 20 personalized cold emails/week to developers/PE firms
Use the cold email script from the playbook
Volume target: 80 emails sent, expect 4–8 responses, 1–2 meetings
Film 2–3 spec RE videos (friend's property, AirBnB, commercial space)
Post spec videos to YouTube with SEO titles
Repost clips to Instagram Reels/TikTok
Send direct mailer to 30 local developers (postcard + portfolio link, ~$100)
Phase 2 Budget: ~$800 (mailers, LinkedIn Premium, sample shoot costs)  |  Success Metric: 3+ discovery calls booked, 1+ proposal sent

Weeks 9–10: Proposals & Negotiation

Follow up on all proposals sent (3–5 touch rule)
Send 3+ proposals (use tiered pricing: Essential / Signature / Enterprise)
Handle objections with prepared responses
Offer “founding client” pricing: 30% below market for first 3 clients in exchange for testimonial + 2 referrals
Pricing ladder: Project 1–3 at $900–1,500 → Project 4–6 at $2,500–3,500 → Project 7+ at market rate

Weeks 11–12: First Projects & Systems Test

Close first 1–2 clients
Execute first project end-to-end (solo shoot, owner edit or contract editor)
Document what worked and what didn't
Collect testimonial + case study permission
Ask for 2 referrals from each client
Update portfolio with new work
Begin migration conversation: “Many of our clients move to a monthly retainer after the first project...”
Phase 3 Budget: ~$700 (editor costs for first projects, travel)  |  Success Metric: 1–3 projects closed, $3,000–8,000 revenue, 2+ testimonials

Red Flags

Failure Modes to Watch

Every plan has failure points. Know them in advance so you can dodge them.

1

Week 3 Panic

“I don't have any clients, this isn't working.” → Normal. Keep outreach volume high. Trust the system.

2

Pricing Too High Too Soon

First 3 projects should be below market to build portfolio and social proof. Raise rates after you have case studies.

3

Editing Bottleneck

If you're editing everything, you can't sell. Use a contract editor after project 3 to free up your time for business development.

4

Chasing Bad Clients

If they negotiate hard on price, they'll negotiate hard on revisions. Walk away. Bad clients cost more than no clients.

5

Perfectionism

Launch the website before it's perfect. Send the email before it's perfect. Done beats perfect. Speed is your advantage.
