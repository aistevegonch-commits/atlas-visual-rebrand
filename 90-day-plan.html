Phase 1

Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

Build the legal, financial, and operational backbone. You cannot sell from a broken foundation.

Phase 1 Progress0%
Week 1: Legal & Banking
May 19-25 • 5 tasks • 14 hrs estimated
Form LLC and get EIN 4 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Without this, you can't open a business bank account. Your personal assets stay exposed.
If you skip this: You'll commingle funds and create tax hell. The IRS does not care that you were "too busy."
Get liability insurance quote and bind coverage 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: One lawsuit from a trip on a shoot location ends your company before it starts.
If you skip this: You're gambling with everything. Good luck explaining that to your family.
Open business checking account 2 hrs Difficulty 1
Why this matters: Separating business and personal money isn't optional. It's the bare minimum.
If you skip this: Your accountant will charge you double to untangle the mess at year-end.
Set up accounting system (Wave or QuickBooks) 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: If you don't track expenses from day one, you'll lose deductions and overpay taxes.
If you skip this: You'll have no idea if you're profitable until it's too late to fix.
Register domain and set up professional email 2 hrs Difficulty 1
Why this matters: A Gmail address tells prospects you're not serious. Perception is reality.
If you skip this: Prospects will judge you. They won't tell you why they ghosted. They'll move on.
Week 2: Portfolio & Positioning
May 26 - Jun 1 • 4 tasks • 27 hrs estimated
Build portfolio page with 3 best past projects 8 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: No one hires without seeing work. Your reel is your resume.
If you skip this: You'll have nothing to show prospects. Conversations will die at "Can you send examples?"
Create 1 spec video for real estate niche 12 hrs Difficulty 5
Why this matters: You need something that screams "I understand this industry." Generic work won't cut it.
If you skip this: You'll look like every other generalist. Generalists starve. Specialists get hired.
Write service descriptions and pricing 4 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: If you can't articulate what you sell and for how much, you can't sell it.
If you skip this: Every sales conversation becomes an awkward negotiation. You'll undercharge out of fear.
Set up CRM (HubSpot free or similar) 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Leads slip through cracks without a system. You will forget to follow up.
If you skip this: You'll lose warm leads because you forgot who they were. Happens every time.
Week 3: Prospecting Prep
Jun 2-8 • 4 tasks • 18 hrs estimated
Create LinkedIn company page and optimize personal profile 4 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Prospects will look you up. What they find determines whether they reply.
If you skip this: Your cold outreach will convert at half the rate. You're making it harder for no reason.
Build list of 50 target prospects in Clark County 6 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: Spray-and-pray outreach gets ignored. Targeted lists get replies.
If you skip this: You'll waste hours messaging people who were never going to buy. Quantity without focus is noise.
Research local real estate developer associations and meetups 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Warm introductions beat cold outreach every time. These rooms are full of buyers.
If you skip this: You'll compete in the inbox while your competitors shake hands at events. They will win.
Create pitch deck / one-pager 5 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: When you're on a call, you need something to send that sells while you listen.
If you skip this: You'll fumble through verbal explanations. Prospects need to see it, not hear it.
Week 4: Operations & Team
Jun 9-15 • 5 tasks + reality check • 13 hrs estimated
Set up editor relationships (interview 3 editors) 4 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: You can't edit and sell at the same time. You need reliable editors before you need them.
If you skip this: Your first project will bottleneck on editing. You'll miss deadlines and look amateur.
Create contract template 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Verbal agreements fail. Scope creep destroys margins. A contract is your only protection.
If you skip this: You'll get burned. Everyone does. The client who "seems nice" will demand 12 revisions.
Set up invoicing system 2 hrs Difficulty 1
Why this matters: If you can't invoice professionally, you can't get paid professionally.
If you skip this: You'll send Venmo requests and look like a side hustle. Clients pay slower when you look small.
Create project intake questionnaire 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Bad projects start with unclear expectations. A questionnaire forces clarity upfront.
If you skip this: You'll discover the client wanted drone footage on delivery day. Too late.
Warm network blitz: message 20 contacts 2 hrs Difficulty 1
Why this matters: Your existing network is the fastest path to revenue. They already trust you.
If you skip this: You're ignoring the lowest-friction leads you have. Pride is expensive.
Week 4 Reality Check: You will feel like you haven't accomplished anything real. This is normal. The foundation is invisible but everything depends on it. Don't quit because you can't see progress yet.
Phase 2

Outreach (Weeks 5-8)

Turn on the outbound engine. Cold email, LinkedIn, networking, and content — all in parallel.

Phase 2 Progress0%
Week 5: Cold Outreach Begins
Jun 16-22 • 4 tasks • 15 hrs estimated
Send 20 personalized cold emails to target prospects 6 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: Cold email is a numbers game with a human filter. Personalized gets replies. Templates get deleted.
If you skip this: Your pipeline stays empty. No outreach = no revenue. Period.
Attend 1 local networking event 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: One handshake is worth 50 cold emails. People buy from people they met.
If you skip this: You'll hide behind your screen and wonder why no one knows your name.
Post 3 valuable LinkedIn posts about video marketing for real estate 4 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: Content builds authority before you ask for a meeting. It warms up cold prospects.
If you skip this: Your LinkedIn will be a ghost town. Prospects will see no activity and assume you're inactive.
Follow up on all Week 5 emails that didn't respond 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: 80% of sales happen after the 5th contact. Most people quit after one.
If you skip this: You're leaving money in someone else's inbox. Follow-up is where deals live.
Week 6: Double Down
Jun 23-29 • 4 tasks • 13 hrs estimated
Send 20 more personalized cold emails 6 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: Momentum compounds. Stopping now resets your effort to zero.
If you skip this: Prospects forget you exist. Outreach is like a flywheel — it takes consistent force.
Schedule 2 coffee meetings with potential referral partners 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Referral partners (agents, architects, contractors) are force multipliers.
If you skip this: You'll rely entirely on your own effort. Partners 10x your reach for minimal cost.
Create and send 1 "value-first" email to your network 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Give before you ask. Share a market insight or helpful resource. People remember givers.
If you skip this: Your network will only hear from you when you want something. That's called using people.
Update portfolio with any new work 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Fresh work signals an active business. Stale portfolios signal a dead one.
If you skip this: Prospects will see the same 3 projects for months. They'll assume you have no clients.
Week 7: Follow-Up & Content
Jun 30 - Jul 6 • 4 tasks • 16 hrs estimated
Send 20 more personalized cold emails 6 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: By now your messaging should be sharper. Use what you learned from Week 5-6 responses.
If you skip this: You'll wonder why the pipeline dried up. The answer is you stopped filling it.
Follow up on all non-responders from Weeks 5-6 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Most people are busy, not uninterested. A polite follow-up gets the meeting.
If you skip this: You're writing off leads who were going to say yes on the third email. Amateur move.
Attend 1 more networking event 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Repetition builds recognition. Show up twice and people start remembering you.
If you skip this: You'll be the stranger at every event. Familiarity breeds trust.
Create case study from best past project 4 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: Case studies sell outcomes, not services. Prospects want proof, not promises.
If you skip this: You'll keep describing what you do instead of showing what you deliver. Descriptions don't close.
Week 8: Push to Close
Jul 7-13 • 4 tasks + reality check • 15 hrs estimated
Send final batch of 20 cold emails 6 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: This is the last pure outreach week. After this, your time shifts to closing conversations.
If you skip this: You'll enter Week 9 with an empty pipeline and panic. Don't do that to yourself.
Follow up on warm leads 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Warm leads cool fast. A week of silence kills momentum.
If you skip this: Your "warm" leads will go cold, then dead. You'll have to start over.
Send proposals to any prospects who showed interest 4 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: Interest without a proposal is just a conversation. You need to ask for the business.
If you skip this: Prospects will assume you're not serious. They'll hire the person who sent the proposal.
Send direct mailer to 30 local developers 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Physical mail stands out in a digital world. Most competitors won't bother.
If you skip this: You're leaving a cheap, high-impact channel on the table. Competitors will take it.
Week 8 Reality Check: If you don't have at least 1 prospect in active conversation by now, your outreach is failing. Change the message, not the volume. Something in your pitch, portfolio, or targeting is off. Fix it before Phase 3.
Phase 3

Close (Weeks 9-12)

Convert conversations into contracts. Execute, deliver, and learn.

Phase 3 Progress0%
Week 9: Nurture & Refine
Jul 14-20 • 4 tasks • 9 hrs estimated
Follow up on all proposals 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Proposals sit in inboxes until someone nudges them. That someone is you.
If you skip this: Your proposals will expire silently. Prospects won't chase you. Ever.
Schedule discovery calls with interested prospects 3 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Discovery calls turn maybes into meetings. You can't close over email alone.
If you skip this: You'll trade 20 emails when one 20-minute call would have closed it.
Refine pitch based on feedback 3 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: If prospects are saying no, they're telling you what's wrong. Listen and adjust.
If you skip this: You'll keep delivering the same broken pitch and getting the same rejection. Insanity.
Ask for referrals from past clients/contacts 1 hr Difficulty 1
Why this matters: Referrals convert at 10x the rate of cold outreach. This is the highest-ROI hour you'll spend.
If you skip this: You're ignoring a channel that costs nothing and closes faster than anything else.
Week 10: Close & Execute
Jul 21-27 • 3 tasks + reality check • 17+ hrs estimated
Close first project (negotiate, contract, deposit) 4 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: Revenue is the only metric that matters. Everything else is preparation.
If you skip this: You'll be two and a half months in with nothing to show. That's not a business. That's a hobby.
If no close: analyze why and adjust 3 hrs Difficulty 3
Why this matters: If you can't diagnose failure, you'll repeat it. Be brutally honest with yourself.
If you skip this: You'll blame the market, the economy, or bad luck. It's none of those. It's your approach.
Execute first project with extreme quality 10+ hrs Difficulty 5
Why this matters: This project will test everything — your planning, your skills, your communication. Nail it.
If you skip this: Your first impression becomes your last. One bad delivery kills word-of-mouth before it starts.
Week 10 Reality Check: Your first project will feel chaotic. That's because it is. Document everything that goes wrong. That document becomes your process manual. Embrace the mess — it's tuition.
Week 11: Deliver & Learn
Jul 28 - Aug 3 • 4 tasks • 5.5 hrs estimated
Deliver first project and request testimonial 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Testimonials are social proof. Without them, your next prospect has no reason to trust you.
If you skip this: You'll have zero proof you can deliver. Your next sales cycle will be twice as hard.
Follow up for referral 1 hr Difficulty 1
Why this matters: Happy clients want to help. You have to ask. They won't offer unprompted.
If you skip this: You're leaving the easiest revenue on the table. One referral is worth 10 cold calls.
Bill final invoice 30 min Difficulty 1
Why this matters: You did the work. Get the money. Invoicing promptly signals professionalism.
If you skip this: Clients won't remind you. You'll do free work and resent them for it. Your fault.
Assess: what worked, what didn't 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Every project teaches you something. If you don't capture it, you learn nothing.
If you skip this: You'll make the same mistakes on project 2, 3, and 4. Waste of time and reputation.
Week 12: Honest Review
Aug 4-10 • 4 tasks + reality check • 7.5 hrs estimated
Review 90-day results honestly 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Lying to yourself is the fastest way to go broke. Numbers don't care about your feelings.
If you skip this: You'll enter the next 90 days with the same broken assumptions. Delusion is expensive.
Update financial model with actual numbers 1 hr Difficulty 1
Why this matters: Projections are fiction until you have real data. Actuals drive real decisions.
If you skip this: You'll keep operating on a fantasy budget. Runway shrinks while you pretend it doesn't.
Plan next 90 days based on reality 2 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Hope is not a strategy. Your next plan must be rooted in what actually happened.
If you skip this: You'll repeat the same 90 days expecting different results. That's the definition of failure.
Celebrate if you have revenue. If not, decide: continue, pivot, or quit. 2.5 hrs Difficulty 2
Why this matters: Both continuing and quitting are valid. Lying to yourself is not. Make the decision clear-eyed.
If you skip this: You'll drift into month 4 with no plan, no revenue, and no direction. That's not persistence. That's denial.
Week 12 Reality Check: Celebrate if you have revenue. If not, decide: continue, pivot, or quit. Both are valid. Lying to yourself is not. Your runway is 5-6 months. Count the weeks remaining. Act accordingly.
